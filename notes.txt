============================= preposition ===============================
hinter -> behind (es steht hinter euch -> it's behind you)
	2-> to the back, nach hinten  (ich gehe hinter)
	hinten -> in the back (adverb) (is the name of a place)(nach/von .. ok)
	hinten is a name of a location and as such you can answer the question 
		Where? by simply saying hinten, given that the context is known.
	Hinter is a preposition and it puts things or persons in a relation to 
		one another. So hinter needs to be followed by a noun or person.
	yourdailygerman.com/2012/02/21/difference-between-hinter-and-hinten/
auf -> on(to)
	accusative: when it speaks about a direction or destination.
	dative: when it indicates a stable position or situation.

	ich war in _der_ moschee -> i was in the mosque
	wir gingen in eine moschee -> we went into a mosque
